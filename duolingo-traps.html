Duolingo Grammar Traps

Konu Bulgarca Türkçe Açıklama Örnek
Say vs Tell Казвам vs Разказвам ‘say’ + something, ‘tell’ + someone She said that she was tired.
She told me she was tired.
Much vs Many Много (небр.) vs Много (бр.) Much → sayılamaz, Many → sayılabilir How much money?
How many apples?
Good vs Well Добър vs Добре Good → sıfat, Well → zarf He is a good teacher.
She sings well.
Too vs Enough Твърде vs Достатъчно Too → aşırılık, Enough → yeterlilik It’s too cold.
She is tall enough.
Since vs For Откакто vs За (период) Since → başlangıç noktası, For → süre I have lived here since 2010.
I have lived here for 10 years.
A vs An един/една/едно (неопределителен член) Ünsüz sesiyle başlayan kelimeler → a
Ünlü sesiyle başlayan kelimeler → an		 a book
an apple
a university (çünkü başında /j/ sesi var)
an hour (çünkü “h” sessiz)
Sayfa mı Sentence mı Страница vs Изречение Page = sayfa, Sentence = cümle Turn to page 5.
This is a long sentence.
Still / Yet / Already Все още / Все още не / Вече Still: hâlâ, Yet: henüz, Already: çoktan She still lives there.
I haven’t finished yet.
I've already done it.
Since vs Because Тъй като vs Защото Since = çünkü (neden), Because = çünkü (açıklama) Since he was tired, he went to bed early.
He went to bed early because he was tired.
Less vs Fewer По-малко (небр.) vs По-малко (бр.) Fewer = sayılabilir, Less = sayılamaz Fewer people attended the meeting.
We have less money now.
Each vs Every Всеки vs Всеки един Each = bireysel, Every = genel Each student received a certificate.
Every student must attend.
Who vs Whom Кой vs Кого Who = özne, Whom = nesne Who is coming?
Whom did you see?
Among vs Between Между (повече) vs Между (двама) Among = 3+ kişi/şey, Between = 2 kişi/şey He stood among his friends.
The prize is between you and me.
Been vs Gone Бил vs Отишъл Been = gidip dönmüş, Gone = gitmiş ve hâlâ orada She has been to Italy.
He has gone to the store.
That vs Which Който vs Който (с доп. инфо) That = gerekli bilgi, Which = ekstra bilgi The book that I bought is great.
The book, which was expensive, is great.
Raise vs Rise Вдигам vs Издигам се Raise bir nesne ister (transitive), Rise kendi yükselir (intransitive). They raised the flag.
The sun rises at 6 AM.
Borrow vs Lend Взимам назаем vs Давам назаем Borrow = ödünç almak, Lend = ödünç vermek. Can I borrow your pen?
I will lend you my car.
Lay vs Lie Поставям vs Лежа Lay = bir şeyi koymak (transitive), Lie = kendiliğinden uzanmak (intransitive) Lay the book on the table.
I want to lie down.
Advice vs Advise Съвет (Същ.име) vs Съветвам (Глагол) Advice = isim, Advise = fiil. He gave me some advice.
I advise you to rest.
In spite of vs Despite Въпреки vs Въпреки Her ikisi “…e rağmen” anlamında, isim/gerund alır. In spite of the rain, we went out.
Despite the warning, he continued.
During vs While През vs Докато During = isimden önce, While = cümleden önce. During the movie, I slept.
While I slept, the show ended.
Either vs Neither Едно от двамата vs Никой от двамата Either = ikisinden biri, Neither = hiçbiri. You can either call or email.
Neither option works for me.
Wish vs If only Искам vs Ако само Wish + past: şimdiki pişmanlık, Wish + past perfect: geçmiş pişmanlık; If only: vurgulu “keşke”. I wish I knew the answer.
If only I had studied harder.
Used to vs Would Used to = навици и състояния, Would = само навици (действия) Used to: geçmiş alışkanlık ve durum, Would: geçmiş alışkanlık eylemleri. I used to live in London.
Every summer we would go to the lake.
Too…to vs …Enough to Too = твърде…за да, Enough = достатъчно…за да Use Too + adj/adv + to-inf for excess and adj/adv + enough + to-inf for sufficiency — Türkçesi: Too aşırı+to-inf, Enough yeterli+to-inf He is too young to drive.
She is old enough to vote.
May vs Might May = вероятност, Might = по-слаба вероятност или минало на may May = olası, Might = daha zayıf olasılık veya geçmişte. It may rain later.
She might have missed the train.
Unless vs If…not Unless = ако не (отрицателно условие) Unless = “if not” (…madıkça/…olmazsa). I won’t go unless you come.
He’ll fail if he doesn’t study.
Not only… but also След Not only… but also се инвертира глагол–подлог за акцент Vurgu için inversion (Not only did she… but also…). Not only did she win the prize, but she also broke the record.
Not only is he talented, but he is also humble.
So… that / Such… that So + прилагателно + that или Such + съществително + that за резултат So + adj + that / Such + noun + that → sonuç bildirir. She was so tired that she fell asleep immediately.
It was such a difficult exam that few passed.
Had better Had better + инфинитив (без to) за силен съвет Had better + yalın fiil → güçlü tavsiye. You had better finish your work before you leave.
We had better call a doctor soon.
Modal perfects модал + have + минало причастие за минали възможности/съжaления modal + have + fiilin 3. hali geçmiş pişmanlık/olasılık için You should have told me earlier.
He could have won if he had tried.
I would have come, but I was busy.
Bare infinitive after let/make/help let/make/help се използва инфинитив без to Had better + yalın fiil → güçlü tavsiye. Let me see your work.
She made him apologize.
I helped him (to) move the table.
Mixed conditionals Свързва се минало условие с настоящ резултат или обратното Geçmiş şart + şimdiki sonuç veya tersi birleştirilir If I had studied law, I would be a lawyer now.
If she were here, she would have helped us.
