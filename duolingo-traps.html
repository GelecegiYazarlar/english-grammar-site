Duolingo Grammar Traps

Konu Bulgarca Türkçe Açıklama Örnek
Say vs Tell Казвам vs Разказвам ‘say’ + something, ‘tell’ + someone She said that she was tired.
She told me she was tired.
Much vs Many Много (небр.) vs Много (бр.) Much → sayılamaz, Many → sayılabilir How much money?
How many apples?
Good vs Well Добър vs Добре Good → sıfat, Well → zarf He is a good teacher.
She sings well.
Too vs Enough Твърде vs Достатъчно Too → aşırılık, Enough → yeterlilik It’s too cold.
She is tall enough.
Since vs For Откакто vs За (период) Since → başlangıç noktası, For → süre I have lived here since 2010.
I have lived here for 10 years.
A vs An един/една/едно (неопределителен член) Ünsüz sesiyle başlayan kelimeler → a
Ünlü sesiyle başlayan kelimeler → an		 a book
an apple
a university (çünkü başında /j/ sesi var)
an hour (çünkü “h” sessiz)
Sayfa mı Sentence mı Страница vs Изречение Page = sayfa, Sentence = cümle Turn to page 5.
This is a long sentence.
Still / Yet / Already Все още / Все още не / Вече Still: hâlâ, Yet: henüz, Already: çoktan She still lives there.
I haven’t finished yet.
I've already done it.
Since vs Because Тъй като vs Защото Since = çünkü (neden), Because = çünkü (açıklama) Since he was tired, he went to bed early.
He went to bed early because he was tired.
Less vs Fewer По-малко (небр.) vs По-малко (бр.) Fewer = sayılabilir, Less = sayılamaz Fewer people attended the meeting.
We have less money now.
Each vs Every Всеки vs Всеки един Each = bireysel, Every = genel Each student received a certificate.
Every student must attend.
Who vs Whom Кой vs Кого Who = özne, Whom = nesne Who is coming?
Whom did you see?
Among vs Between Между (повече) vs Между (двама) Among = 3+ kişi/şey, Between = 2 kişi/şey He stood among his friends.
The prize is between you and me.
Been vs Gone Бил vs Отишъл Been = gidip dönmüş, Gone = gitmiş ve hâlâ orada She has been to Italy.
He has gone to the store.
That vs Which Който vs Който (с доп. инфо) That = gerekli bilgi, Which = ekstra bilgi The book that I bought is great.
The book, which was expensive, is great.
Raise vs Rise Вдигам vs Издигам се Use Raise transitively (needs an object) and Rise intransitively (no object) — Türkçesi: Raise bir nesne ister, Rise kendiliğinden olur — Български: Raise = повдигам (нещо), Rise = издигам се сам They raised the flag.
The sun rises at 6 AM.
Borrow vs Lend Взимам назаем vs Давам назаем Use Borrow when taking something temporarily and Lend when giving temporarily — Türkçesi: Borrow ödünç almak, Lend ödünç vermek — Български: Borrow = взимам назаем, Lend = давам назаем Can I borrow your pen?
I will lend you my car.
Lay vs Lie Поставям vs Лежа Use Lay to put something down (transitive) and Lie to recline (intransitive) — Türkçesi: Lay nesne ister, Lie özne kendiliğinden uzanır — Български: Lay = поставям нещо, Lie = лежа сам Lay the book on the table.
I want to lie down.
Advice vs Advise Съвет (isim) vs Съветвам (fiil) Use Advice as a noun and Advise as a verb — Türkçesi: Advice isim, Advise fiildir — Български: Advice = съвет, Advise = съветвам He gave me some advice.
I advise you to rest.
In spite of vs Despite Въпреки vs Въпреки Use either to mean “despite” with a noun or gerund — Türkçesi: Her ikisi de ‘rağmen’ anlamında isim/fiil-ing alır — Български: И двете означават „въпреки“ и следват съществително/герунд In spite of the rain, we went out.
Despite the warning, he continued.
During vs While През vs Докато Use During before a noun and While before a clause — Türkçesi: During isim, While cümle başı için — Български: During = преди съществително, While = преди изречение During the movie, I slept.
While I slept, the show ended.
Either vs Neither Едно от двамата vs Никой от двамата Use Either to talk about one of two and Neither to talk about none of two — Türkçesi: Either iki arasından biri, Neither hiçbirisi — Български: Either = единият от двамата, Neither = нито един от двамата You can either call or email.
Neither option works for me.
Wish vs If only Искам vs Ако само Use Wish + past for present regrets and Wish + past perfect for past regrets — Türkçesi: Şimdiki pişmanlık için Wish + past, geçmiş için Wish + past perfect — Български: За настоящо съжаление – Wish + минало време, за минало – Wish + past perfect I wish I knew the answer.
If only I had studied harder.
Used to vs Would Eskiden vs Alışkanlık geçmiş Use Used to for past habits and states, and Would only for past habitual actions — Türkçesi: Used to durum ve eylem, Would yalnız eylem alışkanlıkları için — Български: Used to = навици и състояния, Would = само навици (действия) I used to live in London.
Every summer we would go to the lake.
Too…to vs …Enough to Çok…için vs Yeterince…için Use Too + adj/adv + to-inf for excess and adj/adv + enough + to-inf for sufficiency — Türkçesi: Too aşırı+to-inf, Enough yeterli+to-inf — Български: Too = твърде…за да, Enough = достатъчно…за да He is too young to drive.
She is old enough to vote.
May vs Might İhtimal vs Daha zayıf ihtimal Use May for a possibility and Might for a weaker possibility or past reference — Türkçesi: May olasılık, Might daha düşük olasılık/ geçmişi için — Български: May = вероятност, Might = по-слаба вероятност или минало на may It may rain later.
She might have missed the train.
Unless vs If…not …madıkça vs Eğer…değilse Use Unless to mean “if not” for negative conditions — Türkçesi: Unless = eğer…olmazsa anlamına gelir — Български: Unless = ако не (отрицателно условие) I won’t go unless you come.
He’ll fail if he doesn’t study.
Not only… but also Не само… но и Use Not only… but also with inversion for emphasis: verb before subject — Türkçesi: Not only… but also yapısında vurgu için özne-fiil yer değiştirir — Български: След Not only… but also се инвертира глагол–подлог за акцент Not only did she win the prize, but she also broke the record.
Not only is he talented, but he is also humble.
So… that / Such… that Толкова… че / Такъв… че Use So + adj + that or Such + noun + that to express result — Türkçesi: So + sıfat + that veya Such + isim + that sonuç bildirir — Български: So + прилагателно + that или Such + съществително + that за резултат She was so tired that she fell asleep immediately.
It was such a difficult exam that few passed.
Had better По-добре е / Yapmalı Use Had better + bare infinitive to give strong advice — Türkçesi: Had better + yalın fiil güçlü tavsiye verir — Български: Had better + инфинитив (без to) за силен съвет You had better finish your work before you leave.
We had better call a doctor soon.
Modal perfects Трябваше да / Можеше да / Щеше да Use modal + have + past participle to talk about past possibilities or regrets — Türkçesi: modal + have + fiilin 3. hali geçmiş pişmanlık/olasılık için — Български: модал + have + минало причастие за минали възможности/съжoления You should have told me earlier.
He could have won if he had tried.
I would have come, but I was busy.
Bare infinitive after let/make/help Yalın infinitive Use bare infinitive (no “to”) after let, make, or help — Türkçesi: Let/make/help ardından yalın fiil kullanılır — Български: След let/make/help се използва инфинитив без to Let me see your work.
She made him apologize.
I helped him (to) move the table.
Mixed conditionals Смесени условни изречения Combine a past condition with a present result or vice versa — Türkçesi: Geçmiş şart + şimdiki sonuç veya tersi birleştirilir — Български: Свързва се минало условие с настоящ резултат или обратното If I had studied law, I would be a lawyer now.
If she were here, she would have helped us.
