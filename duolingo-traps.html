Duolingo Grammar Traps

Konu Bulgarca Türkçe Açıklama Örnek
Say vs Tell Казвам vs Разказвам ‘say’ + something, ‘tell’ + someone She said that she was tired.
She told me she was tired.
Much vs Many Много (небр.) vs Много (бр.) Much → sayılamaz, Many → sayılabilir How much money?
How many apples?
Good vs Well Добър vs Добре Good → sıfat, Well → zarf He is a good teacher.
She sings well.
Too vs Enough Твърде vs Достатъчно Too → aşırılık, Enough → yeterlilik It’s too cold.
She is tall enough.
Since vs For Откакто vs За (период) Since → başlangıç noktası, For → süre I have lived here since 2010.
I have lived here for 10 years.
Sayfa mı Sentence mı Страница vs Изречение Page = sayfa, Sentence = cümle Turn to page 5.
This is a long sentence.
Still / Yet / Already Все още / Все още не / Вече Still: hâlâ, Yet: henüz, Already: çoktan She still lives there.
I haven’t finished yet.
I've already done it.
Since vs Because Тъй като vs Защото Since = çünkü (neden), Because = çünkü (açıklama) Since he was tired, he went to bed early.
He went to bed early because he was tired.
Less vs Fewer По-малко (небр.) vs По-малко (бр.) Fewer = sayılabilir, Less = sayılamaz Fewer people attended the meeting.
We have less money now.
Each vs Every Всеки vs Всеки един Each = bireysel, Every = genel Each student received a certificate.
Every student must attend.
Who vs Whom Кой vs Кого Who = özne, Whom = nesne Who is coming?
Whom did you see?
Among vs Between Между (повече) vs Между (двама) Among = 3+ kişi/şey, Between = 2 kişi/şey He stood among his friends.
The prize is between you and me.
Been vs Gone Бил vs Отишъл Been = gidip dönmüş, Gone = gitmiş ve hâlâ orada She has been to Italy.
He has gone to the store.
That vs Which Който vs Който (с доп. инфо) That = gerekli bilgi, Which = ekstra bilgi The book that I bought is great.
The book, which was expensive, is great.
Raise vs Rise Кaлдърmak vs Йüksелmek English: Raise = transitive (needs object), Rise = intransitive
Türkçesi: Raise nesne alır, Rise kendiliğinden yükselir
Български: Raise = подпомпващ, Rise = покачващ се сам 		They raised the flag.
The sun rises at 6 AM.
Borrow vs Lend Заемам vs Давам назаем English: Borrow = take temporarily, Lend = give temporarily
Türkçesi: Borrow ödünç almak, Lend ödünç vermek
Български: Borrow = взимам назаем, Lend = давам назаем 		Can I borrow your pen?
I will lend you my car.
Lay vs Lie Поставям vs Легнати English: Lay = put something down, Lie = recline
Türkçesi: Lay koymak (nesne), Lie uzanmak (özne)
Български: Lay = поставям (преходен), Lie = лежа (непереходен) 		Lay the book on the table.
I want to lie down.
Advice vs Advise Съвет (isim) vs Съветвам (fiil) English: Advice = noun, Advise = verb
Türkçesi: Advice isim, Advise fiil
Български: Advice = съвет (съществително), Advise = съветвам (глагол) 		He gave me some advice.
I advise you to rest.
In spite of vs Despite Въпреки vs Въпреки English: Both = same meaning, use noun/gerund after
Türkçesi: İkisi de aynı, sonra isim/fiil-ing
Български: И двете = въпреки, следва се съществително/герунд 		In spite of the rain, we went out.
Despite the warning, he continued.
During vs While С течение на vs Докато English: During + noun; While + clause
Türkçesi: During isim, While cümle
Български: During = през (имe), While = докато (цялостно изречение) 		During the movie, I slept.
While I slept, the show ended.
Either vs Neither Едно от двамата vs Никой от двамата English: Either = one of two (positive), Neither = none of two (negative)
Türkçesi: Either ikisinden biri, Neither hiçbiri
Български: Either = един от двамата, Neither = нито един от двамата 		You can either call or email.
Neither option works for me.
Wish vs If only Искам vs Ако само English: Wish + past (present regret), Wish + past perfect (past regret)
Türkçesi: Present için Wish + past, Geçmiş için Wish + past perfect
Български: За настояще – Wish + минало време, за минало – Wish + past perfect 		I wish I knew the answer.
If only I had studied harder.
Used to vs Would Eskiden vs Alışkanlık geçmiş English: Used to = past habits & states, Would = past habits (actions only)
Türkçesi: Used to eylem & durum, Would yalnızca eylem
Български: Used to = навици и състояния, Would = само навици (действия) 		I used to live in London.
Every summer we would go to the lake.
Too…to vs …Enough to Çok…için vs Yeterince…için English: Too + adjective/adverb + to-inf, adjective/adverb + enough + to-inf
Türkçesi: Too aşırılık + to-inf, enough yeterlilik + to-inf
Български: Too = твърде…за да, Enough = достатъчно…за да 		He is too young to drive.
She is old enough to vote.
May vs Might İhtimal vs Daha zayıf ihtimal English: May = possibility, Might = smaller possibility or past of may
Türkçesi: May olasılık, Might daha düşük olasılık veya may’in geçmişi
Български: May = вероятност, Might = по-малка вероятност или минало на may 		It may rain later.
She might have missed the train.
Unless vs If…not …madıkça vs Eğer…degilse English: Unless = if not (negative condition)
Türkçesi: Unless = eğer…olmazsa
Български: Unless = ако не (отрицателно условие) 		I won’t go unless you come.
He’ll fail if he doesn’t study.
